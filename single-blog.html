Top Maintenance Tips to Extend Your Vehicle’s Lifespan

July 19, 2024

Car Features News

These tips not only promise to extend the life of your vehicle but also ensure its peak performance and safety. Plus, we’ll explore how the Openbay+ program can transform the way you approach car maintenance, making it a breeze.

Top Maintenance Tips to Keep Your Vehicle Going Strong

1. Regular Oil Changes Your vehicle’s engine relies on oil to run smoothly and efficiently. To prevent engine wear and harmful sludge buildup, it’s important to get regular oil changes as recommended by the manufacturer. Generally, conventional oil should be changed every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, while synthetic oil can last 7,500 to 10,000 miles. Remember to choose quality oil and filters for better engine protection and longevity.

2. Tire Rotations Rotating your tires on a regular basis helps them wear evenly, which extends their lifespan and keeps your vehicle’s handling in top shape. It’s a good idea to get your tires rotated every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, making it convenient to align this service with your oil changes. Proper tire maintenance not only saves you money but also ensures your safety on the road.

3. Fluid Checks Your vehicle relies on various fluids to operate smoothly, such as coolant, brake flui, transmission fluid, and power steering fluid. Regularly checking these fluids can help you identify leaks and prevent costly repairs later on. Keep an eye out for any discoloration or drops under your vehicle, as they could be signs of issues that need immediate attention.

4. Air Filter Replacemen A clean air filter is essential for keeping contaminants like dust and debris out of your engine. It’s recommended to replace your air filter every 12,000 to 15,000 miles, and even more frequently if you frequently drive in dusty conditions. This simple maintenance task can improve fuel efficiency and engine performance.
